DISTANCE CALCULATION METHOD
====================================================================================

Method Used: Rectilinear Distance (Manhattan Distance)
Formula: distance = |x2 - x1| + |y2 - y1|

Rationale: Manufacturing facilities use grid-based layouts where material 
handling equipment (forklifts, AGVs, conveyors) move along aisles (horizontal 
and vertical paths), not diagonally. This makes Manhattan distance more 
realistic than Euclidean distance.

Example (Year 4):
• B1 center: (0 + 38/2, 23 + 62/2) = (19.0, 54.0)
• A center: (43 + 14/2, 17 + 74/2) = (50.0, 54.0)
• Distance: |50-19| + |54-54| = 31 + 0 = 31 ft

Total Process Flow:
B1→A (31 ft) + A→B2 (31 ft) + B2→C (31 ft) + C→D (31 ft) + D→I (52 ft) + I→J (68 ft)
= 244 ft per trip (identical for all three years)
